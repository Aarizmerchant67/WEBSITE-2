What are the 5 Pillars of Islam?

What are they?

Shahada

The shahada are the words 'There is no God but Allah, and Muhammad is his messenger.' This means that Muslims believe that Allah is the sole God and Muhammad(a prophet)is his messenger.

Salah

This means praying 5 times a day: Dawn, just after midday, afternoon, sunset and nighttime.

Zakat

Zakat is when Muslims give a certain amount of charity(2.5%)to the poor. Although, there is a minimum amount of income you earn before having to start charity.

Sawn

Another word for Sawn is fasting. Muslims are expected to fast during the month of Ramadan. Ramadan is once a year. This is to make Muslims understand how people in poverty do and to show gratitude to Allah.

Hajj

Hajj is when Muslims walk around the House of Allah. The House of Allah is in Mecca and they are expected to do Hajj at least a lifetime. However, if you are poor or don't have enough money to afford the trip you are excused to Hajj.

Facts about Islam

Islam is the second-largest religion in the world with only Christianity ahead. The word 'Islam' means submission to the will of Allah. Muslims' holy book is called the Qu'ran. It has two major denomiantions which are Shia and Sunni. Islam is a monotheistic religion, meaning they only have one God. The religion started in 622AD, when Muhammad travelled from Mecca to Medina. Did you know that Allah has 99 different names? Some of the inventions the Islamic world has made are coffee, algebra and the toothbrush. Muslims place of worship is called the Mosque.
